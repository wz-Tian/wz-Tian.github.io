A Data-Driven Solution for Finland Attractions and visitor experience Improving

The aim of this project is to provide recommendations for attraction managers while optimizing the visitor experience

Project Overview

This mini-project focuses on analyzing visitor comments about tourist attractions in Finland. Our goal is to leverage data-driven methods to provide actionable insights that enable tourist attraction management teams to improve their overall performance in a scientific and effective manner.

Objectives

  • Data-Driven Insights: Help management understand visitor sentiment and pinpoint specific areas for improvement.
  • Enhanced Visitor Experience: Use visitor feedback to refine attraction offerings and service quality.
  • Competitive Edge: Improve reputation and competitiveness of Finnish tourist sites.

Background

In our daily travels, we often rely on websites like TripAdvisor or Google Maps to check reviews and ratings of attractions or destinations, helping us choose better places to visit and enhance our travel experience.



However, these reviews are not always straightforward and can often be influenced by their timeliness. (We can see the Reviews on Tripadvisor and GoogleMap here. You know, actually you cannot get the exact infomation you want to have a overview image towards a destination)



As a result, even after carefully reading through each review, it can be difficult to draw clear conclusions, as different people have varying perspectives on the same attraction. And even there are few negative comments, they shouldn’t simply be treated as noise.

Method

​ Our project will utilize sentiment analysis to deeply explore visitor feedback, assisting the management in identifying tourist needs, pain points, and expectations for the attractions. This analysis can provide actionable recommendations based on real data, enabling the attractions to enhance service quality and responsiveness, while gaining a competitive advantage.

Future

We plan to develop a web-based platform, followed by a mobile app. Our initial primary target users are attraction managers and management teams, but in fact, this platform also serves as a more straightforward reference for tourists. It aims to visualize the strengths and weaknesses of tourist attractions from a statistical perspective and provide direct recommendations for improvement to the management.

Moreover, the application operates in real-time, allowing attractions to mark improvements on the website after addressing specific issues. Tourists can then verify these changes by “rating” the improvements, creating a positive feedback loop. In addition to regularly updating the attraction review data, we can also make “regularized” adjustments to our conclusions based on accumulated feedback from tourists and further optimization by the attractions.
